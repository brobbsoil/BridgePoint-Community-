Who We Are

Two crises. One practitioner. A proven methodology.

BridgePoint Community was founded by Nana Sarfo Brobbey — a digital inclusion specialist based in Anaheim, California — to bring a field-tested, human-centred methodology to the two most urgent community challenges in Southern California: elder digital exclusion and at-risk youth disconnection.

🎯
Our Mission

Equip. Protect. Connect.

To deliver structured, accessible, community-embedded digital literacy and engagement programmes that protect elderly Americans from exploitation, connect isolated seniors to the services and family they depend on, and equip at-risk youth with the digital foundation and community infrastructure they need to build productive lives.

🔭
Our Vision

Communities where no one is left behind by the digital world.

A Southern California where every elderly resident can access their government benefits, manage their finances, connect with their family, and recognise online fraud — and where every at-risk young person has the digital literacy, vocational awareness, and community connection to participate fully in the American economy and civic life.

💡
Our Approach

Human-centred. Community-embedded. Outcome-measured.

We do not deliver classroom training and leave. We go where the populations we serve already are — aged care homes, senior centres, libraries, after-school programmes, faith communities — and deliver structured programmes with pre/post assessments, companion mobile applications, and 90-day follow-up to ensure outcomes are real and lasting.

🌍
Our Values

Dignity. Accessibility. Evidence. Community.

Every elderly adult deserves to navigate the digital world with confidence and safety. Every young person deserves direction, connection, and the digital foundation that the 21st-century economy requires. We believe prevention is more effective, more humane, and more economically rational than intervention after harm has already been done.

About the Founder
👤
Nana Sarfo Brobbey
Digital Inclusion Specialist
Anaheim, California

Proved in Ghana. Refined in London. Applied in California.

The methodology behind NAVIGATE and GROUNDWORK was not developed in a classroom. It was built over five years at Ghana Oil Company — Ghana's largest petroleum marketing company — where Nana designed and deployed a human-centred digital adoption programme that helped elderly customers use a mobile payment platform across a national network of 450+ service stations, achieving over 10,000 downloads.

The insight from that work was simple but powerful: the problem is never purely technological. It is human. The right instruction, delivered at the right moment, in the right format, for the right person — that is what turns digital access into digital adoption.

That methodology is now in Anaheim, California. Applied to elderly Americans navigating SSA.gov. Applied to seniors trying to recognise romance scammers. Applied to at-risk youth who need digital literacy to participate in the economy they were born into.

Our Programmes

Two structured programmes. One community ecosystem.

Each programme is delivered directly at the locations where the populations we serve already gather — not in classrooms they must travel to. Each includes a purpose-built companion mobile application.

For Elderly Adults · 6 Sessions
NAVIGATE
Digital Onboarding and Fraud Protection System

A structured 6-session curriculum delivered at senior centres, aged care homes, libraries, community health centres, and faith communities — teaching elderly adults to use digital systems safely and independently, and to recognise and resist the fraud schemes that cost elderly Americans $7.7 billion in 2025.

  • 1
    Your Digital Identity — Staying Safe Online
    Passwords, two-factor authentication, identifying safe websites
  • 2
    Government Services Online
    SSA.gov, Medicare portal, SNAP enrolment, DMV appointments
  • 3
    Healthcare Portals and Telehealth
    Patient portal registration, telehealth appointments, prescriptions
  • 4
    Digital Banking Safety
    Mobile banking, fraud alerts, APP fraud recognition, Zelle safety
  • 5
    Recognising and Resisting Fraud
    Romance scams, tech support scams, government impersonation
  • 6
    Staying Connected Safely
    Video calling, safe social media, reducing isolation digitally
Host NAVIGATE at Your Facility
For At-Risk Youth · 8 Sessions
GROUNDWORK
Community Engagement and Digital Foundation System

A structured 8-session curriculum delivered at after-school programmes, Boys and Girls Clubs, juvenile diversion programmes, schools, and faith communities — giving at-risk young people the digital literacy, vocational direction, mentorship connections, and peer accountability they need to build productive lives.

  • 1
    Who You Are and Where You Are Going
    Values inventory, community mapping, peer group norms
  • 2
    Digital Foundations — Your Online Identity
    Digital footprint, employer background checks, safe social media
  • 3
    The Economy and You
    Digital banking, building credit, avoiding predatory finance
  • 4
    Vocational Exploration — What Is Possible
    Career pathways, trade alternatives, local labour market data
  • 5
    Applications, Resumes, and Interviews
    Online job search, resume building, video interview preparation
  • 6
    Community and Violence — The Real Costs
    Gang statistics, incarceration consequences, decision-making
  • 7
    Navigating Government and Institutions
    Rights, digital government services, interacting with authorities
  • 8
    Building Forward — Your 12-Month Plan
    Goal setting, mentorship matching, peer accountability pairing

How We Work

From first contact to lasting change.

Our delivery model is structured, outcome-measured, and community-embedded at every stage.

1
Partnership Agreement
We formalise a delivery arrangement with your institution — space, schedule, and participant recruitment
2
Pre-Assessment
Participants complete a Digital Confidence Assessment establishing a measurable baseline before sessions begin
3
Structured Delivery
6 or 8 sessions delivered on-site using plain-language instruction, hands-on practice, and the NAVIGATE or GROUNDWORK companion application
4
90-Day Follow-Up
Graduates are contacted at 90 days and 12 months to assess skill retention, goal progress, and ongoing support needs
